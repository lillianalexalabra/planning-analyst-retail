# SKIMS — Company Overview

## Founding and Background

SKIMS is an American shapewear and clothing brand co-founded in 2019 by media personality and entrepreneur Kim Kardashian, alongside Swedish entrepreneur Jens Grede and his wife Emma Grede (Wikipedia, *Skims*). Kardashian had described herself as having "designed shapewear for 15 years" before launching the brand, having long altered foundation garments to suit her own clothes. Per the Kim Kardashian Wikipedia entry, SKIMS is one of several consumer brands she has built — alongside KKW Beauty (2017) and SKKN by Kim (2022) — and is now her largest and most operationally serious business. It is run by Jens Grede as CEO, with Kardashian serving as the creative and brand-equity engine.

The company was originally announced as "Kimono Intimates" in June 2019. After significant public criticism — including a public letter from the Mayor of Kyoto, Daisaku Kadokawa, arguing that the trademark disrespected Japanese culture — Kardashian renamed the company SKIMS and launched on September 10, 2019 (Wikipedia, *Skims*). The first drop sold out within ten minutes and produced more than $2 million in profit, and by the end of its first year SKIMS claimed sales of more than three million units (Wikipedia, *Skims*).

From the start, SKIMS' founding insight was that traditional shapewear was a poorly-served category: limited size ranges, narrow color palettes optimized for one skin tone, and a heritage focus on hiding the body rather than supporting it. SKIMS framed its offering as "solutionwear" with explicit emphasis on body positivity and inclusive sizing. The conceptual lineage of the category — corsets, girdles, bodysuits, and modern compressive knits made from spandex/Lycra blends — is documented on Wikipedia's *Foundation garment* (Shapewear) entry, which positions SKIMS within a centuries-long tradition of body-shaping undergarments while noting that its mass-market scale and tonal range are genuinely new.

## Product Lines

SKIMS is now a multi-category intimates and apparel platform rather than a pure shapewear label. The core shapewear assortment on skims.com is broken into sub-collections — SKIMS Body, Seamless Sculpt, Sheer Sculpt, Sheer Seamless, and Waist Trainers — covering light, mid, firm, and maximum compression levels (skims.com/collections/shapewear). Adjacent categories include bras and underwear (Fits Everybody bras, lightweight cotton briefs, push-up styles), loungewear (Cotton Fleece hoodies and joggers, Soft Lounge sleep sets, Cotton Rib tees, robes, pajamas, and shorts; skims.com/collections/loungewear), and swim (Iconic Swim and Signature Swim bikinis, one-pieces, and cover-ups; skims.com/collections/swim).

Per the Wikipedia *Skims* entry, the brand sells in sizes XXS to 5XL and offers shapewear in nine skin-tone shades — both are differentiators against legacy intimates competitors. In October 2023 SKIMS launched its first menswear collection (boxers, tees, socks, tank tops), expanding the addressable market beyond womenswear (Wikipedia, *Skims*).

## Brand Positioning and Valuation

SKIMS is one of the most successful celebrity-founded direct-to-consumer brands of the past decade, and its valuation trajectory reflects that. Wikipedia's *Skims* entry traces a fundraising path from $1.6 billion (April 2021) to $3.2 billion (January 2022) to $4 billion (July 2023), and reports the brand was valued at over $5 billion as of 2025 following a ~$225 million round led by Goldman Sachs. The company generated approximately $1 billion in net sales in 2024.

Strategically, SKIMS has used three levers to build that valuation: celebrity-driven content marketing (campaigns starring Kardashian, the broader Kardashian-Jenner family, and a roster including Sabrina Carpenter, Rosé, Lana Del Rey, Charli XCX, and Nicola Coughlan); high-profile collaborations and partnerships (FENDI x SKIMS in 2021, Team USA in 2021–2022, NBA/WNBA/USA Basketball as official underwear partner from 2023, and a Nike x SKIMS Spring 2026 collection); and selective wholesale distribution at Nordstrom layered on top of its DTC website (Wikipedia, *Skims*). Together this positions SKIMS as a celebrity-led but operationally serious modern intimates brand competing for share that previously belonged to Spanx, Victoria's Secret, and Hanes.

## Key Milestones

- June 2019 — Brand announced as "Kimono Intimates"; rebranded to SKIMS after public criticism (Wikipedia, *Skims*)
- September 10, 2019 — SKIMS launches; first drop sells out in 10 minutes and produces $2M+ in profit
- 2020 — Sells more than 3 million units in first full year of business
- April 2021 — Reported valuation exceeds $1.6 billion
- 2021 — Team USA partnership for Tokyo 2020 Olympics & Paralympics; Beijing 2022 Winter Games
- October 2021 — FENDI x SKIMS capsule generates an estimated $3M in sales in 10 minutes
- January 2022 — Valuation doubles to $3.2 billion; named to TIME 100 Most Influential Companies
- November 2022 — Receives inaugural CFDA "Innovation Award" presented by Amazon Fashion
- July 2023 — Valuation reaches $4 billion
- October 2023 — Launches SKIMS Mens; named official underwear partner of NBA, WNBA, and USA Basketball
- 2024 — Surpasses approximately $1 billion in net sales
- December 2024 — Nia Long fronts shapewear campaign
- 2025 — Valuation reaches $5+ billion after ~$225M round led by Goldman Sachs
- January 2026 — Launches Nike x SKIMS Spring 2026 collection
