A **foundation garment** (also known as **shapewear** or **shaping underwear**) is an [undergarment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Undergarment "Undergarment") designed to alter the wearer's [body shape](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Female_body_shape "Female body shape"), to achieve a more fashionable figure.

Specific styles of foundation garments have been essential to some [fashion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fashion "Fashion") movements, and were required in some [social](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social "Social") situations during various fashion periods.\[ _[vague](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Vagueness "Wikipedia:Vagueness")_\]

## History

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Foundation_garment&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")\]

The concept of foundation garments dates back millennia, with early forms waist control and bust support being worn to shape and control the body's appearance. In ancient civilizations, such as Greece and Rome, women utilized binding materials to enhance their figures. Over time, [corsets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corset "Corset") and their predecessors, such as laced kirtles, pairs of bodies, and stays, became popular during the Middle Ages and evolved into the Victorian era. Foundation garments have always been used to achieve a specific silhouette, but the desired shapes got more dramatic as technology improved and allowed for [tightlacing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tightlacing "Tightlacing").

Women have worn foundation garments, such as corsets and [brassieres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brassieres "Brassieres"), for a very long time.[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foundation_garment#cite_note-1) Including this mid 20th century foundation garment patented by Marion M Chubby in 1965 (categorized in elastic corsets).[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foundation_garment#cite_note-2)

In the early 20th century, the invention of elastic materials and the introduction of new designs led to the development of modern shapewear. The Roaring Twenties brought about the "flapper" style, which involved a looser silhouette, but foundation garments such as bras, girdles, and slips were still used to control and shape the body.[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foundation_garment#cite_note-3)[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foundation_garment#cite_note-4)[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foundation_garment#cite_note-5) In the mid-20th century, advancements in fabric technology and the women's liberation movement led to more comfortable and practical options for shaping garments.

## Description

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Foundation_garment&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Description")\]

Foundation garments are worn to modify body shape. They usually require technological advances in garment design and lightweight fabrics to create different body figures. A foundation garment may be worn for a specific outfit. Being underwear, the foundation garment should not be visible under the outerwear. [\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foundation_garment#cite_note-6) A general-purpose "all the way" shaper with clear straps that starts at the bust and ends at the knee or mid-calf is also available.

Foundation garments may come with a built-in strapless bra for dresses and halters.

In a broader definition, a foundation garment is a piece of garment that provides body-redefining shapes such as a bra.

Garments may be categorized according to level of shape control offered, for instance, light, medium, or firm. The simplest foundation is a [body-liner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Body-liner "Body-liner") or [bodysuit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bodysuit "Bodysuit"), which is an ultra-light-weight [leotard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leotard "Leotard") and offers a light touch of smoothing. These are available in a [unitard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unitard "Unitard") style (shortened legs) or a [camisole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Camisole "Camisole")-leotard style. The leotard is available in [boy-leg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Panties#Boyshorts "Panties") and [capri-leg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capri_pants "Capri pants") lengths, with [spaghetti straps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spaghetti_strap "Spaghetti strap"), low cut [necklines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neckline "Neckline"), and even scoop backs, to cater to the outerwear under which the garments will be worn. These softly smooth the figure and provide light support. Micro-fiber camisoles and boy-leg briefs or "hot pants" are also available. [Girdles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Girdle_(undergarment) "Girdle (undergarment)") are often called "body shapers" or "contour garments". These garments are made with more Lycra [spandex](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spandex "Spandex") as compared to the 10% Lycra / 90% cotton blend of most leotards, and they offer the highest level of shaping and support.\[ _[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_\]

## Types of foundation garment

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Foundation_garment&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Types of foundation garment")\]

Foundation garments come in a variety of styles and designs to cater to different body shapes and clothing needs. Some common types include:

- Corsets: Traditional corsets, worn around the waist, provide shaping and cinching effects to create an hourglass figure.
- Girdles extend from the waist to the hips or thighs, providing control and shaping for the lower abdomen, hips, and buttocks.
- Control panties are designed to target the lower abdomen and hips, providing a seamless and smooth appearance under clothing.
- Body shapers are one-piece garments that shape the entire torso, including the abdomen, waist, hips, and sometimes the thighs.
- Thigh shapers focus on shaping and smoothing the thighs, providing control and support for a slimmer appearance.

## Fabric and construction

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Foundation_garment&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Fabric and construction")\]

Foundation garments are constructed using various fabrics, depending on the desired level of control, comfort, and breathability. Common materials include nylon, spandex, Lycra, and cotton blends like coutil, canvas, and calico. Many modern shapewear pieces incorporate seamless construction techniques to minimize visible lines under clothing and enhance comfort.
