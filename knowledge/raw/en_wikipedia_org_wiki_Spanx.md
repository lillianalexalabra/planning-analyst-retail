**Spanx, LLC** is an American [shapewear](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shapewear "Shapewear") company founded in 2000 by [Sara Blakely](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sara_Blakely "Sara Blakely") in [Atlanta, Georgia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia "Atlanta, Georgia").[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-Inc-3) The company offers [innerwear](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Innerwear "Innerwear") and [outerwear](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outerwear "Outerwear"), including [activewear](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Activewear "Activewear"), [apparel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apparel "Apparel"), [denim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Denim "Denim"), shapewear, [swimwear](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swimwear "Swimwear"), intimates, [leggings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leggings "Leggings"), and [hosiery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hosiery "Hosiery").[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-4) As of 2021, the company was valued at $1.2 billion and its products are sold in over 50 countries.[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-Sara-5)

## History

### Founding

Sara Blakely created Spanx in 2000 as a solution to the discomfort she experienced wearing [pantyhose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pantyhose "Pantyhose") in hot weather, and disliking the seamed foot that stuck out of an open-toe shoe.[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-OConnor2-6) After graduating from [Florida State University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florida_State_University "Florida State University") (FSU) in 1993, Blakely first worked at [Walt Disney World Resort](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walt_Disney_World_Resort "Walt Disney World Resort"), followed by Danka, an [office supply](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Office_supply "Office supply") company, where she sold [fax machines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fax_machines "Fax machines") door-to-door. She cut the feet off her control-top pantyhose to achieve a “smooth look” under white pants but did not like that the pantyhose rolled up her leg. Blakely moved to [Atlanta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlanta "Atlanta"), where she continued working for Danka from 9-5, set aside her $5,000 in savings, and spent two years creating her product.[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-OConnor2-6) Blakely explained that she chose the name “Spanks” because of the “k” sound in the internationally recognized “ [Coca-Cola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coca-Cola "Coca-Cola")” and “ [Kodak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kodak "Kodak").”[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-7) She changed the spelling to “Spanx” at the last minute after reading that made-up names sold better.[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-OConnor3-8) After several hosiery factories turned down her product, Blakely brought her idea to [Neiman Marcus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neiman_Marcus "Neiman Marcus"), where she convinced the buying rep by modeling her white pants with and without Spanx.[\[9\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-9) She then brought the product to [Bloomingdale’s](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bloomingdale%E2%80%99s "Bloomingdale’s"), [Saks Fifth Avenue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saks_Fifth_Avenue "Saks Fifth Avenue") and [Bergdorf Goodman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bergdorf_Goodman "Bergdorf Goodman").[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-OConnor3-8)

Once the Spanx brand was created, Blakely sent several of her products to [The Oprah Winfrey Show](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Oprah_Winfrey_Show "The Oprah Winfrey Show"). In November 2000, the brand was featured in [Oprah's Favorite Things](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah's_Favorite_Things "Oprah's Favorite Things").[\[10\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-10) In 2001, Blakely arranged with QVC to feature her product.[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-OConnor3-8) In 2011, Spanx reported revenue of just under $250 million,[\[11\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-OConnor1-11) and Forbes rated Blakely as the youngest self-made female billionaire.[\[11\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-OConnor1-11) Spanx opened its first [brick-and-mortar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brick_and_mortar "Brick and mortar") store in 2012.[\[11\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-OConnor1-11) In 2013, [Jessica Alba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jessica_Alba "Jessica Alba"), [Adele](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adele "Adele") and [Jennifer Garner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jennifer_Garner "Jennifer Garner") wore Spanx to the [Oscars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oscars "Oscars").[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-12) [Gwyneth Paltrow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gwyneth_Paltrow "Gwyneth Paltrow") also wears the brand.[\[13\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-13)

In 2021, the asset manager firm Blackstone bought a [majority stake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Majority_stake "Majority stake") in Spanx, valuing the company at $1.2 billion.[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-Gottfried-14) An all-female investment team from Blackstone prepared the deal,[\[15\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-15) and it was announced that the [Board of Directors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Board_of_Directors "Board of Directors") would be all-female, with Blakely serving as [executive chairman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Executive_chairman "Executive chairman").[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-Gottfried-14) At the same time, Oprah Winfrey, [Reese Witherspoon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reese_Witherspoon "Reese Witherspoon"), and [Whitney Wolfe Herd](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whitney_Wolfe_Herd "Whitney Wolfe Herd") also invested in Spanx.[\[16\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-16)

### Launch

In 2000, Blakely launched the Spanx brand from her apartment,[\[17\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-17) undertaking all initial calls and marketing herself. Her boyfriend at the time, a healthcare consultant, later resigned from his job and joined Blakely in the running of the nascent business.[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-Inc-3)

Blakely previously conducted a meeting with a representative of the [Neiman Marcus Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neiman_Marcus "Neiman Marcus"), at which she changed into the product in the ladies restroom in the presence of the Neiman Marcus buyer to prove the benefits of her innovation.[\[18\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-18) Blakely's product was sold in seven Neiman Marcus stores as a result of the meeting;[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-Inc-3) [Bloomingdales](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bloomingdales "Bloomingdales"), [Saks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saks_Fifth_Avenue "Saks Fifth Avenue"), and [Bergdorf Goodman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bergdorf_Goodman "Bergdorf Goodman") soon followed.[\[19\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-ForbesTurned5000-19)

### 2000 Onwards

Following the establishment of the company, Blakely's then-boyfriend eventually became Spanx's [chief operating officer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chief_operating_officer "Chief operating officer") (COO) and met [Laurie Ann Goldman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laurie_Ann_Goldman "Laurie Ann Goldman") at the [Saks Fifth Avenue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saks_Fifth_Avenue "Saks Fifth Avenue") in Atlanta in 2001, while she was on maternity leave from her employer at the time, Coca-Cola. Goldman was specifically looking for a Spanx product, and the pair exchanged contact details—Goldman became the [CEO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CEO "CEO") of Spanx in 2002.[\[20\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-For-20)

Writing for _[Fortune](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fortune_(magazine) "Fortune (magazine)")_ magazine in February 2014, Colleen Leahey identified Goldman as a key aspect of the company's successful growth:

> Goldman crafted a business model for the company based on lessons she learned during her 10-year stint at Coke: thinking big, starting small, and scaling fast. She advised her team at SPANX to focus on product quality over profit margins. “Every time somebody puts on a SPANX product, one of two things can happen: Our brand can get stronger, or our brand can get weaker. We gain leverage or we lose leverage.”[\[20\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-For-20)

In the first half of 2014, Blakely worked on building the company's first standalone retail stores in shopping malls along the [East Coast of the U.S.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States "East Coast of the United States"), and also introduced denim to an expanding Spanx product line for the "Fall" range in March.[\[21\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-Pow-21)

Goldman resigned from the company in February 2014, after over 10 years at the head of the Spanx.[\[20\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-For-20) Gregg Ribatt, who had previously worked as an executive for Bennett Footwear, Stuart Weitzman Holdings and Collective Brands' Performance & Lifestyle Group, was appointed as interim [chief executive officer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CEO "CEO"). Ribatt's appointment generated discussions about a potential footwear expansion by Spanx according to a statement Blakely made in October 2013 explaining her ambition to design the world's most comfortable high-heel shoe prior to retirement.[\[22\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-Car-22)

In Blakely's June 2014 _[Forbes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forbes "Forbes")_ profile, the publication claims that the company generates "over $250 million in annual revenues and net profit margins estimated at 20%", while Blakely is very strongly committed to maintaining the private status of Spanx.[\[21\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-Pow-21) However, speculation arose about a possible [IPO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Initial_public_offering "Initial public offering") following Goldman's resignation.[\[22\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-Car-22)

Spanx appointed Jan Singer, previously with [Nike](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nike,_Inc. "Nike, Inc."), as CEO in 2014.[\[23\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-23)

It was announced on October 20, 2021, that Blakely had sold a majority stake in the business to [Blackstone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Blackstone_Group "The Blackstone Group").[\[24\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-24)[\[25\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-25) The company was valued at $1.2 billion.[\[26\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-26) The deal was prepared by an all-female investment team from Blackstone, and it was announced that the [Board of Directors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Board_of_Directors "Board of Directors") would be all-female, including Blakely as the Executive Chairwoman.

In 2025, Spanx and Blakely were included in _[Pirouette: Turning Points in Design](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pirouette:_Turning_Points_in_Design "Pirouette: Turning Points in Design"),_ an exhibition at the [Museum of Modern Art](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Museum_of_Modern_Art "Museum of Modern Art") (MoMA) featuring "widely recognized design icons \[...\] highlighting pivotal moments in design history."[\[27\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-27)[\[28\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-28)[\[29\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-Feeney-2025-29) The company's "OnCore" high-waisted mid-thigh short has been in the MoMA design collection since 2017.[\[30\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-30)

## Products

Spanx has a range of sizes, from XS-3X.[\[31\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-31) The company has expanded from shapewear, intimates and hosiery to apparel, activewear,[\[32\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-32) loungewear,[\[33\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-33) and denim.[\[34\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-34) Their core collections include SPANXshape Booty Boost,[\[35\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-People1-35)[\[36\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-36) SPANX Air Essentials,[\[37\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-37) SPANXsculpt Seamless Power, SPANXsculpt OnCore,[\[38\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-38) and SPANXshape EveryWear SPANXsmooth PerfectFit.[\[35\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-People1-35)[\[39\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-39)

## Corporate affairs

### Ownership

Spanx is a private company and has not released financial information. Forbes estimates its value at $540 million in 2021.[\[40\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-40)

In 2002, [Laurie Ann Goldman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laurie_Ann_Goldman "Laurie Ann Goldman"), formerly at [Coca-Cola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Coca-Cola_Company "The Coca-Cola Company"), was appointed [CEO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CEO "CEO") of Spanx.[\[41\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-41) She stepped down in 2014 and was replaced by Jan Singer, [Nike](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nike,_Inc. "Nike, Inc.")’s head of apparel.[\[42\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-42) In 2024, Caroline (Cricket) Whitton was appointed as CEO and Jeanne Jackson as executive chair of the board.[\[43\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-43)

To celebrate the company's $1.2 billion deal with Blackstone, employees received two [first-class](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_class_(aviation) "First class (aviation)") plane tickets and $10,000 each.[\[44\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-44)

Blakely has also expanded beyond Spanx with the launch of her footwear company, Sneex.[\[45\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-45)[\[46\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanx#cite_note-46)
